minimalX - A Beautiful and User-centered Pelican Theme

Gepostet am Sa, 19. November 2016 in Tech
#pelican #theme #webdesign

Tumblr, Blogger or WordPress are propably the most popular platforms on the web to create a personal or professional blog. Offering free hosting, beautiful themes, good social media integration and more, it is super convenient for bloggers to set up their personal web presentation.

However, for me it didn't feel right to just write something and publish that on a preconfigured platform. It felt kind of too done. And doing things differently is always a good idea. It may cost you more effort – but the result can be awesome. And you learn a lot of new stuff.

When I was looking for an alternative way to create my own blog, I came across Pelican – a Python toolkit which generates static web pages out of simply formatted text files. I found the concept of it interesting and there is an active community that contributes to themes and plugins that further extend the core functionality of Pelican.

Own Pelican theme?
Challenge accepted!

As for the theme, I was looking for a fresh and modern design that focuses on what's most important for the user in his current situation. It should have a pleasant look and be easy to navigate. After scrolling through most of the currently available themes on Pelicanthemes, I didn't exactly find the perfect hit. Most themes were too text-intensive and seemed technical in its design and structure.

As a consequence, I accepted the challenge to create my own theme. HTML and CSS weren't completely new to me, I had some experience from coding my own website as a teenager.

One of my inspirations has been the blogging platform Medium. They do some things very well in my opinion: They focus on simplicity and ease-of-use both for bloggers and readers. As for the design, they make use of fonts that are easy to read and implement a harmonious color palette. Also, articles are designed to be enriched by images and videos which makes the content stand out more. Of course, Medium acts as more than just a simple blog hoster, they are basically a social platform.

So what I wanted for my theme is to provide a clear and consistent design that focuses on the user's needs. It has to adhere to formal usability standards and should contribute to a nice overall user experience. No matter what device is used to visit my blog – it has to look good and focus on my content. Also, I wanted to do better with tags and categories.

I started with some paper prototyping to draft my first ideas of the landing page of my blog. It consists of a simple but recognizable page head, some short introduction of the blog purpose and author, followed by cards with articles in chronological order. After the most recent article, a tag cloud shows all appearing topics. The amount of information presented to the user has been reduced to a minimum on this prototype to focus on the most important: the blog posts.

Paper prototyping is useful in early phases of interaction design. This picture shows some basic ideas of the landing page design.

Finally I am happy to present you minimalX – a visually appealing and user-centered Pelican theme. The code for this theme is MIT licensed and available for free on Github. Feel free to share and modify! If you have any comments on the basic design, I'll be happy to respond to your comments below. Since minimalX is far from perfect, I continue to further improve this theme. Issues and pull requests are welcome on Github.

Design focus

  • Visually appealing design
  • Clear and usable design, simple & intuitive navigation
  • Focus on what's really important – your articles. Presented in a clean and straightforward way
  • Make tags great again!
  • Native integration of media - headerimages, gallerys / videos

Features

  • Minimal & flat design
  • Fully responsive and mobile-first
  • Real Tagcloud
  • Disqus / Piwik integration
  • Share articles via privacy-friendly social media buttons
  • W3.CSS CSS framework & HTML5 semantics
